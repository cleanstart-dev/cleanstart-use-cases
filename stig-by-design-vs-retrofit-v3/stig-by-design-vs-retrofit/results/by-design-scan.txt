============================================================
What honest reporting looks like for the by-design result:
============================================================

Expect SOME failures even on a well-hardened image. Don't be tempted
to massage the numbers. Common legitimate failures on container scans
that are NOT image defects:

- Host-kernel sysctl values the container cannot set or observe
- AppArmor profile inheritance (depends on host config)
- Persistent audit log storage (depends on volume mounts at deploy time)
- Audit daemon service-enablement state (host responsibility)
- GRUB / partitioning / BIOS rules (don't apply to containers at all)

Per the DISA Container Hardening Process Guide, an assessor is
expected to evaluate the validity of each failure, not just count them.
A credible comparison post does the same.
